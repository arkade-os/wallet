Arkade Wallet — Offline Recovery

This is a self-contained recovery tool for your Arkade wallet. It runs entirely on your device and makes no network requests — you can (and should) use it with your internet connection turned off. Keep a copy of this file with your backup.

Your keys never leave this page. To verify: disconnect from the internet before typing your recovery phrase, or check the network tab of your browser's developer tools.

Recover with your passkey (no seed phrase)

Wallets secured with a passkey can be recovered without the 12 words — even if the website is gone or seized. Passkeys only work for the wallet's domain, so this page must be served at that domain from your own machine.

This page is opened from disk, so the passkey can't be used directly. Serve it at your wallet's domain with the rescue server below (desktop only), then follow the printed steps.
How it works / manual steps

1. On the computer and browser profile where the wallet was used, download the script above (needs Node.js and openssl or mkcert).
2. Run it next to this file: sudo node arkade-rescue-server.mjs arkade-recover.html --host <your-wallet-domain>
3. It prints the remaining steps: add a hosts entry pointing the domain to 127.0.0.1 (this also blocks the real site), trust the local certificate, and open the printed address in the same browser.
4. This page then reappears served at the wallet's domain: your encrypted wallet is found automatically and your passkey decrypts it. Undo the hosts entry and certificate trust afterwards.
